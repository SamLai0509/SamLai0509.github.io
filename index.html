Zhisen Lai | PhD Researcher

University of Nebraska Omaha.

Omaha, NE

I am a PhD student in Computing & Information Science at the University of Nebraska Omaha. My research focuses on developing AI-enhanced scientific compression and ROI-aware data reduction frameworks to optimize storage and reconstruction for large-scale HPC simulations.

I am also dedicated to advancing physics-informed machine learning architectures to improve the denoising and detection of ultra-high-energy particles. During my time with the GRAND experiment, I built a deep convolutional denoiser for antenna traces that achieved a median output-SNR gain of 15-23 dB.
